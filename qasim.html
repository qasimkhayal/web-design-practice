What is the Solar System?

🌍 The solar system is our cosmic neighborhood, anchored by the powerful Sun at its center. 🪐 It includes eight diverse planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and mysterious dwarf planets. ☄️ These celestial bodies orbit the Sun, forming a dynamic, ever-moving cosmic dance. 🌌 It’s a tiny part of the universe—but it’s where every Earth story begins. The Sun is the colossal star at the center of our solar system, holding 99.8% of its total mass. It’s primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, constantly undergoing nuclear fusion to produce immense energy. This energy powers life on Earth and drives our planet’s climate and weather systems. The Sun’s surface temperature is around 5,500°C, while its core blazes at over 15 million°C. It rises in the east and sets in the west, gifting us daylight and breathtaking sunrises and sunsets. Solar flares and sunspots are just a few of the dynamic phenomena that occur on its surface. Without the Sun’s steady heat and light, Earth would be a frozen, lifeless rock. From mythology to science, the Sun has always captured human imagination as a symbol of vitality and power.
